Web & UI Design

There are many skills to learn and paths to branch under the hood of UX design. Some designers may gravitate more towards the engineering side of things and some to the more visual side. Even if you aren't planning to be doing visual design, it never hurts to have a solid understanding of how to create appealing and delightful interfaces. Here are some great reads to help both those who are curious and those who are eager to become great at the UI side of things.

UI is Communication: How to Design Intuitive, User Centered Interfaces by Focusing on Effective Communication

Everett N. McKay

UI design encompasses many complicated aspects and it's easy to get lost in all the challenges of it. This book is a very complete guide on how to effectively design interfaces and what goes into the process by providing a myriad of examples and techniques.

HTML and CSS: Design and Build Websites

Jon Duckett

Brush up on your coding skills with Jon Duckett's extremely easy to understand book on HTML and CSS. Many other books on web development are intimidating and uninspiring for newcomers, and it doesn't have to be that way. This book provides simple illustrations and intuitive examples that will help get your coding skills of the ground.

Seductive Interaction Design: Creating Playful, Fun, and Effective User Experiences

Stephen P. Anderson

The art of seduction is effective in UI design and can make or break user engagement with a product. Through principles of psychology and tons of great examples, this book will help you create products that users can't put down.

Designing Interface Animation: Meaningful Motion for User Experience

Val Head

Animation in UI design is what truly makes interfaces feel alive and human. You don't need any prior knowledge or experience in animation, this book is an excellent crash-course on how to use animation to make truly great products.

The Humane Interface: New Directions for Designing Interactive Systems

Jef Raskin

This book is a unique guide to creating interactive design systems that are focused on the most effective principles in UI design.

Interaction of Color: 50th Anniversary Edition

Josef Albers

Josef Albers is a pioneer and important figure in the world of color perception and influence. This classic book is an excellent resource for learning the importance of color in design and how to use it effectively.

Web Form Design: Filling in the Blanks

Luke Wroblewski

Written by a top Google designer, this book explores Luke Wroblewski's influential secrets of form design and usability. You'll learn everything you need to know about how to design effective forms and take the pain out of the process of filling them.

Simple and Usable Web, Mobile, and Interaction Design

Giles Colborne

This book really drives home the fact that products that are easy to use win the favor of customers. A great read on simplicity, usability, UI design, and tying it all together.

Design Basics Index

Jim Krause

An essential guide to visual design for designers of any skill-level. This is one of the most attractive books ever printed, and serves as great inspiration. This is one you'll have on your desk forever.

Thinking with Type

Ellen Lupton

A definitive guide to typography and incredibly useful resource. You'll come out of it with a deep understanding of how typography can drive visual communication and how to use it effectively.
