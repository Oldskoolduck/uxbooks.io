UX Fundamentals

Whether you are a beginner who seeks to learn essential knowledge and methodologies or a seasoned veteran in the field looking to brush up on the fundamentals of UX, these are some of the best and important books to help you in any part of your journey.

The Design of Everyday Things

Don Norman

Delightfully entertaining, eye-opening, and insightful, cognitive scientist Don Norman's essential book is a must-read for anyone interested in how and why products either frustrate or satisfy the user. This book brilliantly changes your perspective of the world. You'll never see a door handle or a light switch the same way again.

Don't Make Me Think

Steve Krug

Steve Krug breaks down the fundamentals and issues of digital design into an incredibly concise and simple format. This book really nails the idea that if a website isn't usable, people won't use it and will go elsewhere with their money. An absolute must-read for anyone interested in designing interfaces and what makes a product delightful to use.

The Elements of User Experience

Jesse James Garrett

One of the most important books for beginners and a vital reference for web and interaction design. This book will give you an overall foundation for design and establish important principles to follow in everything you create.

Rocket Surgery Made Easy

Steve Krug

An incredible way to get started and learn the fundamentals of usability testing, one of the most important skills for UX designers. Steve Krug gives great practical and simple directions on how to usability test effectively in this book. It will leave you inspired and ready to get out there and test!

A Project Guide to UX Design

Russ Unger & Carolyn Chandler

It's one thing to learn essential knowledge and fundamentals, but putting it into practice in the real world is equally as important. This book defines user experience design and it's role in the project life cycle and how it fits into the software development ecosystem.

About Face: The Essentials of Interaction Design

Alan Cooper

A must have reference guide for any UX designer. This book is credited as evolving the landscape of interaction design, and rightfully does so. Think of it as an in-depth encyclopedia of everything to with with UX that also provides great examples and design methods.

The User Experience Team of One: A Research and Design Survival Guide

Leah Buley

UX designers often face situations where they are the lone wolf or don't have nearly enough time to do what needs to be done. Leah Buley's book will prepare you for any situation by teaching you lightweight, practical, and collaborative methods to help you make the biggest impact on any project.

100 Things Every Designer Needs to Know About People

Susan Weinschenk

To be a great designer is to truly understand people. This invaluable book provides eye-opening information that will help both new and experienced UX designers really understand who they are designing for and how to anticipate human actions and feeling.
